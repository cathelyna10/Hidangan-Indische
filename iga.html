HIDANGAN INDISCHE





Originating from the vibrant culinary traditions of East Java, Indonesia, "Iga Penyet" translates directly to "smashed ribs." This dish offers a unique fusion of flavors and textures that embodies Indonesian cuisine. Tender beef ribs are first simmered in a rich blend of spices, including shallots, garlic, turmeric, and ginger, until they're fall-off-the-bone soft. After boiling, they are briefly fried to achieve a golden, crispy exterior. The "smashing" process involves gently flattening the ribs, enhancing their tenderness and ensuring flavors penetrate deep into the meat. Served with a fiery sambal—a spicy chili paste with hints of shallots, garlic, and tomato—and often accompanied by steamed rice and fresh vegetables, Iga Penyet is a flavorful dish that offers a delightful dance of heat, savory notes, and textural contrasts on the palate. Whether consumed in bustling street-side eateries or upscale restaurants, this dish stands as a testament to Indonesia's rich culinary heritage




Iga Penyet with Sambal



Ingredients:

For the Ribs:

  • 500g beef ribs
  • 1 stalk lemongrass, bruised
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp tamarind paste
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp white pepper
  • 500 ml water

Spice Paste:

  • 4 shallots
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 3 candle nut
  • 3 dried chillies, soaked in warm water
  • 1 thumb-sized turmeric, roasted (or 1 tsp turmeric powder)
  • 1 thumb-sized ginger

For the Sambal:

  • 10 red chillies
  • 5 bird's eye chillies (adjust based on your heat preference)
  • 4 shallots
  • 4 candle nut
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 1 tomato, halved
  • a pinch of fermented shrimp paste (terasi)
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil

Instructions:

  1. Preparing the Ribs:
    1. Blend all the spice paste ingredients until smooth.
    2. In a pot, combine ribs, spice paste, lemongrass, bay leaves, tamarind, salt, pepper, and water. Boil until the ribs are tender, approximately 1 hours.
    3. Remove the ribs and set aside. Keep the broth for other uses or as a soup.
    4. Heat some oil in a frying pan and fry the ribs until they are golden brown. Set aside.
  2. Preparing the Sambal:
    1. Blend chillies, shallots, and garlic in a food processor until coarse.
    2. Heat oil in a pan and sauté the chili mixture until aromatic.
    3. Add the tomato, salt, and sugar. Continue to cook until the sambal sauce thickens. Adjust seasoning to taste.
  3. Place the fried ribs in a mortar and gently "smash" them to flatten a bit (this is where the term "penyet" comes from).
  4. Serve the ribs with sambal on the side, steamed rice, and perhaps some slices of cucumber and tomato for freshness.
