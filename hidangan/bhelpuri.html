A quintessential street snack hailing from the vibrant shores of Mumbai, Bhel Puri is an explosion of flavors and textures that dance on the palate. A delightful amalgamation of puffed rice, crispy sev, tangy tamarind chutney, spicy green chutney, and a medley of fresh vegetables like onions and tomatoes — it's a dish that exemplifies the spirit of Indian chaat. Boondi and other crunchy elements often make their way into the mix, enhancing the cacophony of crunches with every bite. But what truly sets Bhel Puri apart is its versatility; additions like mini masala idli or spiced peanuts can be seamlessly integrated, offering unique twists to the classic. Served in a cone of newspaper or a plate, often garnished with fresh coriander and an extra drizzle of chutney, Bhel Puri is more than just a snack — it's an experience, a celebration of flavors, and a testament to India's rich culinary diversity. Whether savored on bustling Mumbai beaches or recreated in kitchens worldwide, its charm remains undiminished.




Bhel Puri



Ingredients:

Main Ingredients:

  • 2 cups puffy basmati rice (puffed rice)
  • 1 cup sev (crispy gram flour noodles)
  • 1 cup mini masala idli
  • 1/2 cup boondi (fried chickpea flour balls)

Additional Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup chopped onions
  • 1/2 cup chopped tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup chopped coriander (cilantro)
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped (optional for heat)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind chutney
  • 1 tablespoon green chutney
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/2 teaspoon chaat masala

Instructions:

  1. Preparing the Bhel Mix:
    1. In a large mixing bowl, combine puffy basmati rice, sev, boondi, and mini masala idli.
    2. Add the chopped onions, tomatoes, green chilies, and coriander to the mix.
  2. Seasoning and Final Mix:
    1. To the mixing bowl, add tamarind chutney, green chutney, lemon juice, salt, and chaat masala.
    2. Mix well, ensuring the ingredients are evenly combined and coated with the chutneys and spices.
  3. Serve immediately in bowls or on plates, garnishing with additional sev, coriander, and a drizzle of chutney if desired. It's essential to serve bhel puri promptly as it loses its crunchiness over time.
