HIDANGAN INDISCHE





Hailing from the culinary heart of Japan, Wagyu Donburi, often referred to as "Wagyu Don," is a luxurious dish that showcases the pinnacle of Japanese beef. Centered around the prized Wagyu beef, known globally for its unrivaled marbling, buttery texture, and rich flavor, this dish pairs thinly sliced or cubed beef with perfectly steamed Japanese rice. The beef is typically simmered in a delicate blend of soy sauce, mirin, sake, and sugar, culminating in a balance of savory umami and subtle sweetness. The result is a harmonious bowl where the velvety, melt-in-your-mouth beef complements the simplicity of the rice. Often garnished with elements like pickled vegetables, crispy tempura bits, or even a soft onsen egg, Wagyu Donburi not only tantalizes the taste buds but also offers an indulgent experience that encapsulates the precision and elegance of Japanese cuisine. Whether savored in a bustling Tokyo eatery or a high-end dining establishment, it's a testament to the unmatched quality of Wagyu beef and the artistry of Japanese culinary traditions.




Wagyu Donburi with Onsen Egg



Ingredients:

  • 150g thinly sliced Wagyu beef
  • 2 cups steamed Japanese rice
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon mirin
  • 1 tablespoon sake
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 green onion, sliced
  • Nori seaweed, for garnish
  • 1 onsen egg (see below for preparation)
  • 1 lotus root, thinly sliced into chips
  • Cooking oil, for frying

Instructions:

  1. Preparing the Onsen Egg:
    1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Once boiling, turn off the heat and add cold water to bring the temperature to precisely 160째F (70째C).
    2. Carefully lower the egg(s) into the water using a slotted spoon. Ensure the egg is fully submerged.
    3. Cover the pot and let the egg sit for about 30 minutes, maintaining the water temperature at 160째F (70째C).
    4. After 30 minutes, remove the egg and immediately submerge it in an ice water bath for a few minutes to halt the cooking process.
    5. Peel the egg carefully, as the white will be quite soft and delicate.
  2. Heat the cooking oil in a frying pan and fry the thinly sliced lotus root chips until they're golden brown and crispy. Set them aside on a paper towel to drain excess oil.
  3. In another pan over medium heat, mix the soy sauce, mirin, sake, and sugar. Stir until sugar is dissolved.
  4. Add the thinly sliced Wagyu beef to the pan and cook until it's browned but still tender.
  5. Place the steamed rice in a bowl, and top it with the cooked Wagyu beef, crispy lotus root chips, and the onsen egg.
  6. Garnish with green onions and nori seaweed.
  7. Serve hot and savor the flavors of your delightful Wagyu Donburi!
